What is the TikTok and Reels Safe Zone?

The safe zone for TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts refers to the central area of a 9:16 vertical video where text, graphics, and important visual elements will not be hidden or cropped by the platform's native user interface (UI). Because platforms frequently update their app layouts, using a 2026 safe zone visualizer ensures your captions and calls-to-action remain perfectly visible.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I keep text safe on Instagram Reels?

To keep text safe on Instagram Reels, you must avoid the bottom 20% of the screen (where your username and caption appear) and the right 11% (where the like, comment, and share buttons are located). Upload your draft to our visualizer to check exact placement.

Are TikTok and Reels safe zones the same?

No, they are different. TikTok has a much larger "danger zone" at the bottom (blocking nearly 37% of the screen for long captions) compared to Instagram Reels. It is highly recommended to visualize your edits for each specific platform before exporting.
